Educator activity overview

Reading Comprehension

See how learners read a complete text, identify its central message, make inferences, and support ideas with evidence.

What learners will do

Read, think, and explain

The learner reads a grade-appropriate story presented in short, accessible sections. After reading, the learner completes three comprehension checks connected directly to that text.

Each check presents a question with several alternatives. The learner chooses an answer, receives clear feedback, and continues through the Reading Journey.

Completion and academic accuracy remain separate, so an incorrect answer does not erase participation or reduce journey progress.

Skills learners develop

Reading comprehension growth

  • Identify the main idea of a complete text.
  • Distinguish important details from minor details.
  • Combine text clues with prior knowledge.
  • Make reasonable inferences.
  • Select evidence that supports an answer.
  • Explain thinking with information from the text.
  • Build confidence through positive feedback.
