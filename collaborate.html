Educator activity overview

Collaborative Discussions

See how learners share ideas, listen carefully, and discuss a reading with respectful academic language.

What learners will do

Talk about the text

Learners respond to a question connected to their reading and use a sentence starter to organize their thinking.

They listen to another idea, respond respectfully, and refer to a detail from the text when explaining agreement or a different point of view.

The activity emphasizes participation, wait time, supportive language, and confidence rather than speed or public comparison.

Skills learners develop

Communication and comprehension

  • Express an idea in a complete sentence.
  • Listen and respond to another perspective.
  • Use evidence during a discussion.
  • Ask relevant follow-up questions.
  • Agree or disagree respectfully.
  • Build speaking confidence in English.
  • Develop collaborative problem-solving habits.
Visual reference

Example discussion

Learner A

“My idea is ___ because the text says ___.”

Learner B

“I agree and would also add ___.”

Follow-up

“Which detail helped you think that?”

Share Ideas

Listen Respectfully

Refer to the Text

Learners support spoken ideas with information from the reading.
