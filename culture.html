Educator activity overview

Culture, Community, and Voice

See how learners connect a reading with family knowledge, languages, traditions, and community experiences.

What learners will do

Connect text and life

Learners identify an idea, event, place, or tradition from a reading and connect it with something meaningful in their own family or community.

They may compare languages, recipes, celebrations, stories, or ways people support one another. A short reflection helps them explain both similarities and differences.

Personal sharing uses aliases and learner choice. The activity does not require sensitive family information.

Skills learners develop

Identity and literacy growth

  • Make text-to-self and text-to-community connections.
  • Compare experiences, traditions, and perspectives.
  • Use descriptive vocabulary in context.
  • Explain how culture shapes stories.
  • Value multilingual knowledge and family voices.
  • Write a focused personal reflection.
  • Build belonging and confidence as a reader.
